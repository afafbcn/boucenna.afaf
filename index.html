My Portfolio

Minimum Cost Tension Problem

In my project, I tackled the Minimum Cost Tension Problem in operations research, which involves minimizing costs while adhering to tension constraints in a network. Using exact methods, I formulated the problem and developed a Python-based solution. This solution efficiently optimized costs while ensuring network stability. Additionally, I designed a user-friendly interface with HTML and CSS, enabling easy interaction and visualization of the optimized results.

Stock Management

Effective stock management is crucial for businesses to avoid issues like overstocking and stockouts. Our application addresses this challenge by offering users a range of replenishment options tailored to their needs, including uniform discounts, finite feed rates, multi-product replenishment, and a default option. This ensures optimized inventory control and streamlined operations, enhancing overall business efficiency and profitability.

Vehicle Routing Problem (VRP)

Our VRP application is a powerful tool designed to tackle the complex challenge of delivery route planning for vehicles. By leveraging advanced algorithms, it efficiently calculates optimal routes while considering factors such as customer locations, delivery demands, vehicle capacities, and time constraints. Users have the flexibility to choose between different constraints and resolution methods, allowing them to tailor the solution to their specific needs. Whether utilizing exact or approximate methods, such as ant colony or genetic algorithms, our application ensures efficient resource utilization and cost minimization for enhanced logistics operations.

Truck Loading Problem(Bin Packing)

Our Bin Packing application is tailored to solve the complex problem of efficiently utilizing containers or boxes to pack a set of differently sized items. Users are offered a selection of different resolution methods, including First Fit Decreasing (FFD) and Extreme Point. Additionally, the application allows users to choose between heterogeneous or homogeneous fleet types, enabling them to select the option that best suits their needs. With these features, our application ensures optimal packing solutions, contributing to improved logistics and resource utilization efficiency.

Combining Bin Packing with VRP

Our Bin Packing combined with VRP application is engineered to address the intricate combined problem of organizing delivery routes for each customer while optimizing the loading of their requests by efficiently managing container utilization. Users are presented with the option to choose a resolution method: VRP Extreme Point First Fit Decreasing. This method ensures that delivery routes are optimized, leading to efficient resource allocation and enhanced logistics operations.
